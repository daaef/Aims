What is Out Grower System?

Out-grower schemes (often referred to as contract farming in academic and other literature) are an important component of many current public-private partnerships (PPPs) in developing countries, including the G8â€™s New Alliance.

Such schemes often appeal to farmers because the company often provides inputs and production services. In addition, farming incomes can rise and such schemes often open up new markets and provide new technology.

Out-grower schemes offer access to land that would not otherwise be available to a company and can also be a disguised form of land grab

Reasons to use Out Grower System

producers

Efficiently organize, manage and monitor large numbers of farmers to improve productivity and reduce risks.

production

Cooperatives, agribusinesses and agricultural processors can obtain real-time information on yield forecasts and harvests to make post-harvest operations easier to manage.

compliance

Increase traceability and reduce auditing costs associated with compliance to food safety and sustainability standards.
